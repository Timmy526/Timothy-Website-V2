    Hello! My name is Timothy Tu. I am 18 years old and will be a freshman attending SUNY Polytechnic Institute in the fall. I will be majoring in Computer and Information Sciences. I am a Chinese/Vietnamese American (My mother is from China and my father is from Vietnam). I have worked hard to keep a high grade point average throughout the school years (ending high school with a 94.78 gpa.). In my freetime, one of my favorite hobbies is to do photography.

    My aim is to study computer science in order to create things such as applications to allow for people with mental health to get the help they need. Although in the world we have therapists and such, there is still a large number of people who feel as though they cannot speak to their parents to get help or even then maybe it is easier for them to get the help they need through talking to strangers. I have come from a background of having to be what is called a Model Minority which is just a term used to describe Asians as being hardworking and never needing help. Although it can be seen as a positive thing, it also is very negative because it means that if something is wrong, it feels wrong to ask for help. I have also been a victim of many instances of bullying which has caused me to feel like I was alone in the world and fearful to ask for help from others. But I have created the strength for myself to fight through it and become stronger for myself, but to also feel that the world needs more help to become better. I have spent time using applications which allow you to speak to people and I feel that those apps (although may not be for everyone), are very helpful because it gives you the feeling like you can speak to people and get help. However, I feel like we should create more apps like it and improve upon it to help everyone. The world is a beautiful place but we need to help fix the problematic parts of it so we can all enjoy the beautiful scenery of life.
